# Prompt 4: Local SEO + Social + Paid Ads System

## Instructions
Run this prompt to generate a 12-month discovery and acquisition engine. Outputs cover Google Business Profile, organic social, paid ads, and influencer outreach — all in one run.

---

## The Prompt

You are a restaurant digital marketing strategist specializing in local SEO, social media, and paid advertising. Generate a complete discovery and paid acquisition system for the restaurant below. Every output must comply with FDA, FTC, Meta, Google, and TABC advertising rules at generation time.

**RESTAURANT DETAILS:**
- Restaurant name: [NAME]
- Cuisine type and concept: [E.g., "wood-fired Neapolitan pizza, Neapolitan-trained chef"]
- Location: [STREET ADDRESS, CITY, STATE, ZIP]
- Neighborhood/district: [E.g., "Green Valley Ranch, Henderson NV"]
- Target keywords (if known): [E.g., "best pizza Henderson" — or leave blank to generate]
- Alcohol license: [Yes / No]
- Average check: $[AMOUNT]
- Primary competitors in the area (name 1–3): [NAMES or "unknown"]
- Budget available for paid ads (monthly): $[AMOUNT or "under $500" / "$500–$1,500" / "$1,500+"]
- Business goal this quarter: [E.g., "fill weekend tables," "grow catering," "launch brunch service"]

**GENERATE ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:**

**1. Google Business Profile Post Calendar (12 weeks, weekly)**
For each of the next 12 weeks provide:
- Post type (What's New / Offer / Event / Product)
- Headline (58 chars max)
- Body (1,500 chars max — aim for 150–200 words)
- CTA button: [Book / Order online / Call / Learn more / Visit]
- Image description (what to photograph)

Seasonal/event anchors to include: Mother's Day (2nd Sunday May), Memorial Day, Father's Day, 4th of July, Labor Day, Halloween, Thanksgiving Eve (biggest bar night of year), holiday season, Valentine's Day, Super Bowl Sunday, St. Patrick's Day.

Compliance: No health claims, no "best in [city]" without attribution, no alcohol promotion language in Google posts (Google policy prohibits).

**2. Local SEO Landing Page Copy**
Target keyword: "[CUISINE TYPE] restaurant [CITY/NEIGHBORHOOD]" (generate 2–3 keyword variants)

Page structure:
- H1 (60 chars): "[CUISINE TYPE] Restaurant in [NEIGHBORHOOD], [CITY]"
- Meta title (60 chars): "[NAME] — [CUISINE TYPE] in [NEIGHBORHOOD] | [CITY, STATE]"
- Meta description (155 chars): cuisine + signature dish + hours + "Reserve a table"
- Intro paragraph (100 words): cuisine, chef/concept story, neighborhood connection
- Menu highlights section (3 items, 30 words each): sensory copy, no health claims
- Hours & parking section (structured for schema)
- Customer quote section (2 quotes, attributed to first name + neighborhood — FTC: quotes must be real or labeled as examples)
- Schema FAQ (JSON-LD, 6 questions): "What are your hours?", "Do you take reservations?", "Is parking available?", "Do you have vegan/gluten-free options?", "What is your price range?", "[CUISINE TYPE]-specific FAQ question"

**3. Facebook & Instagram Ad Copy**
For each format provide: primary text, headline, description, CTA button, compliance notes.

Ad Set A — Awareness (new to restaurant):
- Primary text (125 chars): lead with food/experience, no "click here," no health claims
- Headline (40 chars): curiosity + location
- Description (30 chars): reinforcing detail
- CTA: [Learn More / See Menu]
- If alcohol: do not show drinking imagery; must use age-targeting (21+) in audience settings (Meta policy)

Ad Set B — Offer/Conversion (drive reservation or order):
- Primary text (125 chars): specific offer + expiry date (FTC: no "limited time" without end date)
- Headline (40 chars): offer + call to action
- Description (30 chars): terms or reinforcing detail
- CTA: [Book Now / Order Now]
- Offer structure: bounce-back (% off next visit) NOT "free" (avoids state coupon law risk)

Ad Set C — Retargeting (visited website or FB page, no conversion):
- Primary text (125 chars): social proof angle ("Join 400+ regulars who...")
- Headline (40 chars): overcome objection (price, occasion, parking)
- Description (30 chars): reassurance
- CTA: [Book Now / Get Directions]

**4. Google Search Ad Copy (RSA Format)**
15 headlines (30 chars each) — no single character over limit:
- 3 branded (restaurant name)
- 4 cuisine/concept
- 4 location-specific (neighborhood + city)
- 2 social proof (ratings, years in business, awards with source)
- 2 CTA ("Reserve Tonight," "Order Now — Free Delivery" only if delivery is actually free)

4 descriptions (90 chars each):
- 1 concept/ambiance
- 1 menu highlight
- 1 offer or reason-to-act
- 1 location + hours

Compliance: No "best [city]" without attribution, no superlatives without source, no health/diet claims, no "guaranteed" language.

**5. Nextdoor Neighborhood Post Template**
Nextdoor tone: community member, not advertiser. No promotional language in first paragraph.

- Opening: community connection (neighborhood, local sourcing, years in area)
- Body (100–150 words): what makes you different, invitation to visit — not an ad pitch
- Offer (if any): frame as "neighbor special" not "sale" (Nextdoor algorithm suppresses overtly promotional posts)
- Close: "Hope to see you soon — [OWNER FIRST NAME]"

**6. Food Influencer Outreach Email**
FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides compliance:
- Subject (under 50 chars): genuine value proposition, not "collab request"
- Body (150–200 words):
  - Compliment specific work (requires influencer name/handle — use [INFLUENCER HANDLE] placeholder)
  - Clear offer: complimentary meal for [party size] in exchange for content (FTC requires disclosing this as compensation)
  - No "honest review" language (FTC 2023: implies other reviews are not honest)
  - State expected deliverable: "one Instagram post and one Reel" — be specific
  - No performance requirement: do NOT offer payment tied to post likes, reach, or bookings (FTC: performance-based compensation in exchange for endorsement requires disclosure)
  - Disclosure brief: "Posts featuring our restaurant should include #ad or #sponsored per FTC guidelines — we'll brief you on this before your visit"
- Sign-off: owner/GM name, title, phone

**7. Event Promotion Kit (for 1 specific event)**
Event type: [TRIVIA NIGHT / WINE DINNER / LIVE MUSIC / HOLIDAY SPECIAL / BRUNCH LAUNCH — choose one]
Event details: [DATE, TIME, TICKET PRICE if any, CAPACITY]

Generate:
- Facebook Event description (500 words): hook, what to expect, logistics, FAQ, ticketing (if applicable)
- Email blast (subject + 200-word body): urgency + FOMO + CTA
- Social post (3 versions: 3-week out teaser, 1-week countdown, day-of reminder)
- SMS announcement (under 160 chars, TCPA compliant)
Alcohol events: no "unlimited drinks" language, no "open bar" without specifying duration/limits (TABC/state ABC rules), include "must be 21+" if alcohol is a feature.
