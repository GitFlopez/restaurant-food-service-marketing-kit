# Prompt 1: Grand Opening & New Customer Acquisition Kit

## Instructions
Copy and paste this prompt into Claude (Sonnet or Opus recommended). Fill in every [BRACKET] field before running. The output will be ready to use — do not run through a lawyer before publishing. Compliance is built into the generation.

---

## The Prompt

You are a restaurant marketing expert and local SEO specialist. Generate a complete grand opening and new customer acquisition kit for the restaurant below. Every output must be publish-ready — no "consult a lawyer" placeholders. Enforce all compliance requirements at generation time.

**RESTAURANT DETAILS:**
- Restaurant name: [NAME]
- Cuisine type and concept: [E.g., "Farm-to-table American diner, scratch kitchen, Nevada-sourced ingredients"]
- Location (city, neighborhood, state): [CITY, NEIGHBORHOOD, STATE]
- Seating capacity: [NUMBER] (or "food truck" / "catering only")
- Alcohol license: [Yes — full bar / Yes — beer and wine / No]
- Years in business: [New opening / X years]
- Owner/GM name: [NAME]
- Signature dish or drink: [ITEM]
- Price range: [$ / $$ / $$$ / $$$$]
- Hours: [E.g., "Tue–Sun 11AM–10PM, closed Monday"]
- Website: [URL or "not yet launched"]
- Health inspection score (if available and above 90): [SCORE or "omit"]
- Unique selling point: [E.g., "only wood-fired pizza oven in Henderson" or "20-year family recipe"]
- Target customer: [E.g., "Henderson families, young professionals, date-night couples"]

**GENERATE ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:**

**1. Google Business Profile Description (750 characters max)**
- Open with the cuisine type and location
- Include the signature dish, unique selling point, and price range
- End with hours and a soft call to action ("Reserve your table" or "Walk-ins welcome")
- Do NOT use: "best in [city]" without attribution, "world-famous," "award-winning" without source, or any claim not stated in the restaurant details above
- Keyword-optimize for: "[cuisine] restaurant [city]", "[neighborhood] [cuisine]", "[signature dish] [city]"

**2. Grand Opening Email Announcement**
- Subject line (under 50 characters): excitement + location + specific date if provided
- Preview text (under 90 characters)
- Body (200–300 words): story of the restaurant, what to expect, opening offer
- Opening offer format: bounce-back discount (e.g., "20% off your second visit when you dine with us opening week") — NOT "free item" (avoids state coupon law complications)
- CTA: reservation link or walk-in invite
- Footer: [RESTAURANT NAME] | [ADDRESS] | Unsubscribe: [LINK] | [CITY, STATE ZIP]
- CAN-SPAM compliant: unsubscribe placeholder and physical address required

**3. Grand Opening SMS Campaign (3 messages)**
TCPA compliance required in every message:
- Message 1 (Day-before teaser, under 160 chars): excitement + date + opt-out instruction
- Message 2 (Opening day, under 160 chars): "We're open!" + address + hours + opt-out
- Message 3 (Opening week follow-up, under 160 chars): bounce-back offer + expiry date + opt-out
Format each as: [RESTAURANT NAME]: [message text] Reply STOP to opt out.

**4. Review Request Sequence (2 messages)**
FTC 2023 Endorsement Guides compliance:
- No conditional language ("leave us a review and get a free appetizer")
- No "honest review" language (implies other reviews are not honest)
- No incentives tied to review content
- Email version (under 100 words): thank you + plain ask + Google/Yelp link
- SMS version (under 160 chars): thank you + plain ask + short link placeholder
Both must feel warm and personal, not automated.

**5. First 5 Instagram/Facebook Posts**
For each post provide: caption (150–200 words) + 15–20 hashtags (mix of local + cuisine + broad)
- Post 1: Behind-the-scenes kitchen/prep photo caption — tell the origin story
- Post 2: Signature dish reveal — sensory language, no health claims (no "healthy," "low-cal," "guilt-free" without FDA substantiation)
- Post 3: Team introduction — owner/chef story, connection to community
- Post 4: Grand opening announcement — date, hours, address, what to expect
- Post 5: First-week offer — bounce-back format, expiry date, clear terms
No alcohol consumption imagery instructions if alcohol license = No.
If alcohol license = Yes: do not show intoxication, do not target under-21 imagery.

**6. Press Release to Local Food Editors (400–500 words, AP style)**
- Headline: "[CUISINE TYPE] Restaurant [NAME] Opens in [CITY] [NEIGHBORHOOD] [MONTH YEAR]"
- Dateline: [CITY, STATE] —
- Lede: who, what, where, when (no opinion, no superlatives)
- Body: concept, signature dish, owner background, community connection, pricing
- Boilerplate: one paragraph about the restaurant
- Contact: [OWNER/PR NAME], [EMAIL], [PHONE]
- ### (end mark)
No "best," "first-ever," "revolutionary" without verifiable source.

**7. Health Inspection Display Guidance**
Based on state = [STATE]:
- If Nevada: NV health card display requirement (NRS 446) — post most recent grade in plain view of customers
- If California: AB 2505 — Letter grade (A/B/C) must be posted at entrance
- If Texas: TEX. HEALTH & SAFETY CODE § 437.020 — post inspection report on request, no mandatory display
- Other states: note that requirements vary; recommend posting voluntarily if score > 90 as a trust signal
- Template sentence for website/GBP: "Our most recent [STATE] health inspection score: [SCORE] ([DATE])." — use only if score provided and above 90.
